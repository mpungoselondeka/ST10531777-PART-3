Impact report

The current water loss in South African municipalities is exacerbated by a significant "reporting lag." Our website is designed to help municipalities react in real time.

The current crisis: the 48-hour lag

Citizen-based reporting relies on manual observation, airtime availability, and call centre operating hours. By the time a leak is logged in the database:

  • Thousands of litres of treated water are lost.
  • Soil erosion around the leak often leads to secondary road damage.

Technical solution: real-time sensor integration

Our website utilises Internet of Things sensor arrays. When a pressure-drop event is detected, the system bypasses manual entry and updates the municipal dashboard immediately.

Proactive maintenance

This website, combined with GIS mapping, helps municipalities keep track of all their infrastructure — which pipes have been installed the longest — so they can predict their next maintenance areas.

Impact on both urban and informal settlements

The Luhlelo foundation has made it easier for people in urban areas as well as those in informal settlements to reach out to their municipalities, which has helped:

  • Reduce water-borne diseases.
  • Hold municipalities accountable.
